Inspired by Nature

Every building becomes a technological tree in an interconnected local ecosystem.

In natural ecosystems, trees and other plants are linked through intelligent underground fungal networks that share information and resources for mutual benefit.

Gaian Grid applies that same systems thinking to electricity. Homes, apartments, and buildings become interconnected energy nodes that can share value, support the local network, and respond more intelligently to changing conditions.

Like plants, buildings with solar act as primary producers, turning the sun’s energy into a usable form that can support a broader ecosystem. Stationary batteries act as herbivores, feeding directly on primary producers when possible, while electric vehicles act as omnivores, drawing energy from both the herbivore batteries and the primary producers.

Who can participate

Built for property owners, tenants, and future solar adopters.

Property owners with solar

Sell cheap green electricity to tenants or the wider grid, earn a return on installed assets, and participate in local flexibility markets.

Homes without solar yet

Still benefit from participation by receiving cheaper local solar during sunny periods and supporting more efficient low-voltage distribution networks.

Tenants and shared buildings

Access lower-cost green electricity without needing to own the roof, while participating in a system that is designed to share value more fairly.
