Nature Inspired Technology for People and the Planet

A regenerative energy platform for people, communities, and the grid.

Gaian Grid is a long-term effort to improve energy equity with modern, nature-inspired technology: an interconnected energy ecosystem where local communities share in the value created by renewable electricity through better visibility, better coordination, and fairer local outcomes.

The Gaian Vision

Accelerate the renewable transition without widening the gap between people.

The transition should widen access to cheaper green electricity and resilience, not widen the wealth gap. Gaian is built around the idea that stakeholder value should be shared locally.

The Gaian Mission

Build the visibility and coordination layer the future grid needs.

Through open infrastructure and voluntary telemetry sharing, Gaian turns connected sites into useful sensor points that help make low-voltage networks more observable and easier to coordinate.

Why This Matters

Distributed grids need local visibility, not upstream guesswork.

Rooftop solar, batteries, EV charging, and flexible demand create fast-changing local network conditions. Without time-series visibility at the edge, it is harder to reduce curtailment, coordinate power flows, and share costs fairly.

How Gaian Works

A connected grid behaves more like a nervous system than a blind wire.

Every shared measurement opens a window into one real point in the network. With enough sensor points, the distribution system can respond to local conditions more intelligently, supporting stability, efficiency, lower curtailment, and better use of renewable electricity already available in the community.

Coordination

Better visibility supports local electricity trading, flexible demand, and virtual power plant participation where nearby nodes are exporting and importing on the same network.

Open Infrastructure

Gaian uses Home Assistant as an interoperability layer because distributed energy is already fragmented across vendors, devices, and protocols. The platform is intended to stay open by design rather than depend on a proprietary gatekeeper.

Privacy Boundary

Public views should show community-level participation and network trends, not expose household identities or raw private telemetry. Transparency only works if people can trust where the boundary sits.

The Long-Term Model

Use network value to deepen local participation, not extract from it.

Gaian is being designed so revenue comes from providing coordination and grid-support value, then circulates back into the local energy ecosystem.

Community Koru

The aim is to reward participants, expand sensor coverage, help more households access cheaper green electricity, and create a reinforcing loop where more participation leads to more visibility, better coordination, and more local value over time.

Nature As Model

The long-term model borrows from ecological succession and nutrient cycling: value should be retained locally so the system can grow denser, more resilient, and more beneficial over time.

Outcome

The destination is a more intelligent, fairer renewable grid where households, communities, retailers, and networks can all benefit from better local coordination.
