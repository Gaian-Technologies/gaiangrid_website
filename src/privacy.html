Gaian Grid Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 12, 2026
Effective Date: April 12, 2026

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Gaian Technologies Limited ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, stores, protects, and shares personal information and related site data when you use Gaian Grid ("Service").

Gaian Grid is a distributed grid insights, network visibility, flexibility, and local energy coordination platform. We use connected-site telemetry and related site metadata to support low-voltage network visibility, feeder-level analysis, grid service applications, and public-facing aggregated insights.

We are committed to transparency and giving you control over your data. This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and how you can manage or delete it.

Where we enter into a separate written agreement with a site owner, site operator, partner, or data provider that specifically governs particular data-sharing, service, or operational arrangements, that written agreement prevails to the extent of any inconsistency for its specific subject matter.

What We Collect

We collect information in two ways: information you provide directly, and information we collect automatically when you use the Service.

In some cases, we also receive site data, site references, and related metadata indirectly from site owners, site operators, partners, supported integration providers, or other authorised data providers in connection with connected-site enrolment, integration, or ongoing operation of the Service.

When you enrol with Gaian Grid, we collect and store the information you submit through the enrollment flow. This includes your email address, optional name, request type, country if provided, and any site metadata you choose to submit through the form. Depending on the country selected, the type of connection, or the nature of the relevant integration or service arrangement, this may include an electricity site reference such as an ICP, NMI, MPRN, MPAN, CPE, CUPS, POD, or EAN code, and related site or network metadata reasonably needed to identify, validate, connect, or contextualise a site within the electricity network.

When you use the enrollment flow, we also store supporting operational data including your IP address, request timestamps, verification-code hash, request status, and any issued invite identifier. We use this data to deliver verification emails, prevent abuse, and operate the enrollment flow.

When you connect telemetry from a site, including via smart sensors, current transformers, power meters, inverter or battery systems, or supported integrations such as the Home Assistant add-on (Gaian Grid Telemetry), we collect and store time-series sensor readings and related telemetry metadata such as selected entity identifiers, state values, device attributes, timestamps, and site-specific operational metadata. Connected sites may include household, commercial, industrial, community, or partner-managed sites.

We may also generate or derive network context from the data you provide, including feeder-level analytics, network visibility outputs, and associations between connected sites and parts of the electricity network using site identifiers, site metadata, and other relevant contextual information. We do not publicly display electricity site identifiers such as ICPs or precise location-linked site data on the public live data page unless explicitly stated.

We also use Cloudflare services on parts of Gaian Grid, including Turnstile on the enrollment form and Cloudflare Web Analytics on public website pages. Through those services, technical information such as IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, and request timestamps may be processed.

We do not track your web browsing outside of Gaian Grid. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to provide, operate, and improve the Service. This includes connecting sites, validating and managing site participation, analysing time-series telemetry across local low-voltage feeders and other parts of the electricity network, associating connected sites with network context using site identifiers and related metadata, and generating insights for network visibility, flexibility, local energy coordination, and other grid service applications.

We use your information to improve the Service by analysing usage patterns in anonymised and aggregated form, fixing bugs and errors, developing new features, and optimising performance.

We use your information for security and fraud prevention, including detecting and preventing unauthorised access, monitoring for abuse or malicious activity, investigating security incidents, and complying with legal obligations.

We use your information to communicate with you by sending verification emails, service updates and notifications, responding to support requests, and notifying you of important changes.

We may use connected-site data and derived outputs to support services for authorised electricity-sector participants, including distribution network operators, distributed service operators, site owners, site operators, and other authorised partners involved in network visibility, flexibility, local energy coordination, or grid service applications. Public-facing dashboards and summaries are intended to use aggregated or de-identified information unless otherwise clearly stated.

If you are in the European Union, we process your data based on several legal grounds. We process data based on our contract with you to provide the Service you signed up for. We process data based on our legitimate interest in improving the Service, preventing fraud, and ensuring security. We process data based on your consent for optional features, which you can withdraw at any time. We process data when we have a legal obligation to comply with laws and regulations.

How We Share Your Information

We want to be absolutely clear: we never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

We share data with trusted service providers who help us operate the Service:

Amazon Web Services (AWS) hosts our infrastructure and is used for application hosting, storage, and verification email delivery. Our primary deployment is configured for Australia, specifically the ap-southeast-2 region.

Cloudflare provides security, performance, and reliability services, including Turnstile on the enrollment form and Cloudflare Web Analytics on public website pages.

These service providers process data on our behalf or under their own service terms in connection with the services they provide to us.

We may share data or derived outputs with authorised electricity-sector participants, such as distribution network operators, distributed service operators, site owners, site operators, or other authorised partners, where reasonably necessary to provide, evaluate, develop, or operate the Service and related grid visibility, flexibility, local energy coordination, or grid service functions. Where appropriate, we do so under contracts, data-sharing arrangements, confidentiality obligations, or other lawful safeguards.

Our public live data pages are intended to show aggregated or de-identified information and do not publicly expose electricity site identifiers such as ICPs or precise location-linked site data unless we explicitly say otherwise or have the necessary permission to do so.

We may disclose your information if required by law, including to comply with legal process such as subpoenas or court orders, to respond to government requests, to protect our rights or property, to investigate fraud or security issues, or to protect the safety of users or the public.

If we are acquired by or merge with another company, your information may be transferred. We will notify you of any such transfer and you can delete your data before the transfer if you choose.

We may share your information for other purposes only with your explicit consent.

Data Security

We protect your data with technical and organisational security measures appropriate to the nature of the data we process.

Data transmitted between your browser and our public services is encrypted in transit using TLS version 1.2 or higher. Telemetry transport between Home Assistant and our MQTT broker is encrypted in transit using TLS.

We restrict access to internal services and administrative interfaces using network boundaries, authentication controls, and service-specific credentials.

Where we process sensitive site identifiers or location-linked metadata, we limit access to personnel, services, and systems that reasonably need that access to operate the Service.

Our development practices prioritise security through code reviews for changes, use of parameterised SQL queries where applicable, and avoiding hardcoded secrets in source code.

We store application data primarily in Australia in Amazon Web Services' ap-southeast-2 region. Some requests also pass through Cloudflare services, which may process technical request data through their network.

We retain different categories of data for different periods depending on their purpose. Enrollment request records, site registration records, telemetry data, and operational logs may be retained for as long as needed to operate the Service, investigate abuse or security issues, comply with legal obligations, or maintain system integrity.

If you ask us to delete your data, we will delete or de-identify the relevant data unless we need to keep it for legal, or security reasons. Backups and storage snapshots may retain data for a limited additional period until they expire or are overwritten in the normal backup cycle.

If a data breach occurs, we will investigate it, take steps to contain and remediate it, and provide any notifications required by applicable law.

Your Rights and Choices

You can request access to your data or request deletion by emailing privacy@gaiantechnologies.com. We will respond within the time required by applicable law.

If your information is inaccurate, you can correct it by emailing privacy@gaiantechnologies.com for assistance.

If you are in the European Union or United Kingdom, you have additional rights under GDPR and UK GDPR. You have the right to access your data and request a copy. You have the right to rectification to correct inaccurate data. You have the right to erasure, also known as the right to be forgotten. You have the right to restrict processing and limit how we process your data. You have the right to data portability to export your data. You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests. Where we rely on consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent.

To exercise these rights, email privacy@gaiantechnologies.com. We will respond within 30 days, though we may extend this to 90 days for complex requests. You can file a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe we have violated your rights. Find your authority at https://edpb.europa.eu/about-edpb/board/members_en.

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act. You have the right to know what personal information we collect, use, and share. You have the right to delete your personal information. You have the right to opt out of any sale of personal information, though we do not sell data. You have the right to non-discrimination, meaning we will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

To exercise these rights, email privacy@gaiantechnologies.com. You may designate an authorised agent to make requests on your behalf. We may ask for verification to prevent fraud.

Children's Privacy

Gaian Grid is not intended for children under 13 years old. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are under 13, do not use the Service or provide any personal information. If you are a parent and believe your child has provided us with personal information, contact us immediately at privacy@gaiantechnologies.com and we will delete it.

If you are between 13 and 18 years old, you must have permission from a parent or legal guardian to use the Service.

We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). If we discover we have collected information from a child under 13, we will delete it immediately.

International Data Transfers

If you are outside Australia, your data will be transferred to and stored in Australia where our servers are located.

Our service providers, including AWS and Cloudflare, may process data in other countries as part of providing infrastructure, email delivery, security, analytics, or network services.

If you are in the European Union or United Kingdom, you retain the privacy rights available to you under applicable law.

Cookies and Tracking

We use Cloudflare Web Analytics on public website pages and Cloudflare Turnstile on the enrollment form. These services may use cookies or similar technologies, or may collect technical request data, to provide analytics, abuse prevention, and security functions.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies.

We currently do not respond to Do Not Track signals, but we do not track you across other websites.

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though this may break essential functionality of the Service.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document. For material changes, we will provide notice by email before those changes take effect.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically. By continuing to use the Service after changes take effect, you accept the updated Privacy Policy.
